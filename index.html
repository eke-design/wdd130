Calabar

Calabar, the capital of Cross River State in southern Nigeria, is a city rich in history and culture. It is home to the Efik people and has been a significant hub for trade since the 17th century. Calabar is renowned for its role in the palm oil trade and its historical importance during the Atlantic slave trade. Today, it is celebrated as a tourism hotspot, offering attractions like the National Museum, the Slave History Museum, and the Cross River National Park. Its vibrant culture, clean environment, and warm hospitality make it a unique destination.
